About

Rethinking migration as a tool of exclusion

My name is Ga Eun Cho (조가은, pronounced GAH-un JOH). I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at Johns Hopkins University and a Graduate Fellow at the Chloe Center for the Critical Study of Racism, Immigration, and Colonialism. I serve as Associate Editor of the Cambridge Politics and Society in East Asia series, and during Fall 2025 I was a Junior Fellow at the International Center for Korean Studies in the Kyujanggak Institute at Seoul National University.

My research spans the Political Economy of Development, Migration and Security, and East Asian Politics and Society. Across these fields, I show that development has been conceived in ways that justify, and even require, mobility and exclusion. I am interested in the connections between state goals — economic development, postcolonial reconstruction, modernization, Cold War survival — and boundary-making strategies such as nation-building, migration, and citizenship policy. In Political Economy, I ask how latecomer states pursue development through population engineering; in International Relations, I ask whether migration and diaspora serve or strain a state's economy, politics, and society; and in East Asian Studies, I examine South Korea's political transition and economic development in light of postcolonialism and the Cold War. I approach these questions through a transnational historical lens, tracing the movements and influence of diverse actors across time and space.

I use a mix of qualitative methods — archival, fieldwork, and interview research — in English, Korean, and mixed Chinese-Korean script. My fieldwork has been supported by competitive grants from the Kyujanggak Institute at Seoul National University, the East Asian Studies program, the Chloe Center for the Critical Study of Racism, Immigration, and Colonialism, and the Department of Political Science at Johns Hopkins University.

I hold an MA in International Relations from Seoul National University and a BA in Asian Studies and Economics from Yonsei University.
